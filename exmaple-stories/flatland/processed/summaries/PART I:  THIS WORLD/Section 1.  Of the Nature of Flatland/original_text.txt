1. The narrator refers to their world as Flatland to help readers from Space understand its nature.  
2. In Flatland, geometric figures move freely on a plane but appear only as straight lines to its inhabitants.  
3. The perspective from which Flatlanders see objects makes it impossible to distinguish shapes, as everything appears as straight lines.  
4. The narrator draws a parallel between the Flatland perspective and how sailors perceive distant lands as lines on the horizon.  
5. In Flatland, the absence of a sun or light for shadows means that all geometric figures, regardless of their shape, appear as straight lines.  
6. The narrator promises to explain later how Flatlanders distinguish between different figures and shifts focus to the climate and houses of Flatland.
